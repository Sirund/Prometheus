The Problem

Indonesia's early warning
is broken at the worst moment.

277 million people live in the most seismically active country on Earth. The systems that are supposed to warn them depend on the same infrastructure that earthquakes destroy first.

📡

The network fails when it matters most

Push notification systems require working mobile data. In a major earthquake, cell towers are among the first infrastructure to fail — often within two to four minutes of the event. By the time an alert reaches a phone via the cloud, the window for protective action has often already closed.

Network outages begin within minutes of a major seismic event — long before most alerts arrive
⚠️

Generic warnings don't save lives

Existing alert apps send template messages: "earthquake detected near you." They ignore the structured data BMKG publishes — magnitude, depth, felt intensity, tsunami potential, exact coordinates. People receive identical messages for a M 4.1 tremor and a M 7.8 disaster. Generic guidance at the wrong moment is worse than no guidance.

BMKG publishes rich structured seismic data that no consumer app uses for AI-grounded briefings
🧠

AI assistance is locked in the cloud

Every LLM-powered app on the market requires a server call. Post-disaster connectivity is unreliable for hours — sometimes days. Any app that depends on an API endpoint to answer "how do I treat this wound" or "which direction should I evacuate" is useless exactly when the question is most urgent.

LiteRT LM enables Gemma 4 fully on-device — no API key, no latency, no network dependency
♿

No voice-first interface exists

In a disaster — dark, smoky, panicked — reading a screen is often impossible. Visually impaired users have essentially no options. High-stress situations demand a hands-free, eyes-free interface where you speak a question and hear an answer. No existing disaster app provides this.

Voice input + spoken output fills a critical gap for 4M+ visually impaired Indonesians and anyone mid-evacuation

Why BMKG — and why it matters
beyond Indonesia

Indonesia's Meteorology, Climatology, and Geophysics Agency (BMKG) publishes free, open seismic and weather data. Prometheus is the first app to pipe this structured data directly into an on-device language model to generate event-specific spoken briefings.

But the architecture is intentionally generic. Any country with an open seismic or meteorological API — Philippines PHIVOLCS, Japan JMA, Nepal NSMC, Turkey AFAD — can replace the BMKG polling layer and inherit the entire offline AI pipeline. Prometheus is a template for developing-country disaster AI, not just an Indonesian app.

The Solution

The entire crisis pipeline,
offline, on your phone.

Prometheus doesn't wait for a server. It fuses BMKG open data with Gemma 4 running on the device itself — alarm, briefing, evacuation guidance, and survival answers within 30 seconds, with zero network dependency.

📡

Poll BMKG open feeds

Continuously reads earthquake and weather feeds. Classifies events by magnitude, depth, felt intensity, and tsunami potential against configurable on-device danger thresholds.

🚨

Trigger alarm + Gemma 4 briefing

On a dangerous classification: audible alarm fires and Gemma 4 generates a spoken emergency briefing grounded in the actual BMKG payload — what happened, what to do, what to avoid.

🗺️

Show evacuation bearing

Pins the epicentre on the map and computes the bearing from it to the user's GPS location — "Evacuate North-East (42°)" — so anyone knows which direction to move without network routing.

🎙

Answer questions offline

Speak a question, hear an answer. Gemma 4 handles survival guidance, first aid, injury assessment from camera, and any post-disaster question — entirely on-device, forever offline.

One model. Every disaster scenario. No internet required.

Gemma 4 (E2B, ~2.4 GB) runs via LiteRT LM with GPU acceleration. After a one-time download, it handles emergency briefings, survival chat, image-to-speech scene description, and hold-to-speak voice Q&A — without a single outbound network request.

The BMKG earthquake context is automatically injected into Gemma's system prompt when a dangerous event is active, so every answer is grounded in what actually happened — not a generic template.

Capabilities

Three tabs. One survival machine.

Every feature runs under a single constraint: it must work when the network is down, your hands are shaking, and you have seconds to act.

📡

BMKG Live Monitor

Polls BMKG earthquake feeds on a configurable interval. Classifies events by magnitude, depth, felt intensity, and tsunami potential. Danger state shifts from normal → warning → danger, triggering the alarm pipeline when thresholds are crossed. Also shows live temperature, humidity, wind, and BMKG nowcast weather warnings by province.

autogempa.json · gempaterkini.json · prakiraan-cuaca · nowcast RSS
🚨

Danger Alarm + Gemma 4 Spoken Briefing

When a dangerous event is classified, an audible alarm fires and Gemma 4 generates a spoken emergency briefing in under 30 seconds. The briefing is grounded in the actual BMKG payload — magnitude, location, depth, felt intensity, tsunami potential — not a generic template. The BMKG earthquake context is also injected automatically into the survival assistant for the duration of the event, so every answer Gemma gives is anchored to the real situation. Delivered via AVSpeechSynthesizer, no screen required.

BMKG payload → Danger Rules → Gemma 4 Emergency Prompt → TTS
🗺️

Evacuation Bearing

Plots the BMKG epicentre on Google Maps. Computes the bearing from the epicentre to the user's GPS position using haversine geometry and displays the cardinal direction: "Evacuate North-East (42°)." Works without routing APIs — just geometry and GPS.

Google Maps SDK · CoreLocation · Haversine bearing
💬

Offline Survival Chat

Streaming conversation with Gemma 4 using a survival system prompt — first aid, shelter, water sourcing, Indonesia hazard awareness. Supports image attachment from camera or gallery, which is analyzed and injected into the query. Persists conversation history across sessions. Works with zero connectivity after download.

Gemma 4 · LiteRT LM · PhotosUI · Vision framework
🎙

Voice Talk Panel

Hold the mic button, speak your question, release. SFSpeechRecognizer transcribes on-device. The camera simultaneously captures a scene frame. Combined voice + camera context goes to Gemma 4 via a one-shot conversation (separate from chat history). Gemma's response is spoken immediately. Eyes-free, hands-free, works offline. Designed for visually impaired users and anyone mid-evacuation.

SFSpeechRecognizer · AVAudioEngine · AVCaptureSession · AVSpeechSynthesizer

Gemma as Universal Disaster AI

Gemma 4 doesn't just warn.
It reasons through the disaster.

Most disaster apps are notification systems. They can tell you something happened. They cannot help you think through what to do next. Gemma 4 on-device changes that fundamentally.

When you ask "how do I treat a deep cut with nothing available," or "is it safe to go back to the building," or "what does this sign say" — Gemma reasons through the question in full context, grounded in the active BMKG event if there is one. This is not a lookup table. It is a reasoning engine that happens to work when the internet does not.

The same capability that works for Indonesia works for any disaster, in any country, in any language. Gemma's general knowledge, combined with BMKG-style local data feeds, creates a model that is simultaneously universal and locally grounded — and replicable anywhere in the developing world that faces seismic or meteorological hazard.

What Makes It Different

Three ideas that make Prometheus
a genuinely new thing.

01

Early warning that works when networks fail

Every existing early warning system for Indonesia depends on the network — push notifications, API calls, cloud AI. All of them fail at the exact moment a major earthquake disables cell infrastructure. Prometheus puts the entire pipeline on the device: BMKG polling, danger classification, Gemma reasoning, alarm audio, and TTS briefing. It gets faster when connectivity disappears, because nothing is waiting for a server.

02

BMKG-grounded AI for the developing world

Most AI assistants answer disaster questions generically. Prometheus injects the actual BMKG structured payload — coordinates, magnitude, depth, felt intensity, tsunami potential — directly into Gemma's emergency system prompt. The response is specific to the quake that just happened beneath you, not a template for "a large earthquake." This pattern — open government data + on-device LLM — is directly replicable in any country with a seismic or meteorological open API, making Prometheus a model for developing-country disaster AI worldwide.

03

Voice-first survival in every scenario

The Talk panel treats voice as the primary interface from the start, not an afterthought. Hold the mic, speak the question, hear the answer — while the camera simultaneously captures the scene for additional context. This serves visually impaired users, people in dark or smoky environments, and anyone mid-evacuation whose hands are occupied. Gemma 4's general reasoning capability means it can handle first aid, injury triage, evacuation planning, and hazard identification from camera input — all in one sentence, spoken back immediately.
